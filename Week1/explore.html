    "CYBER DISTANCE"


    In the reading from the "Critical Atlas of the Internet," it is mentioned that in the digital realm of the internet, the concept of distance undergoes a profound transformation. The internet erases physical distances, making everything just a click away. In this context, the traditional notion of spatial distance becomes irrelevant, as the architecture of the internet is controlled by search engines. However, I have a question regarding this idea of the "distance of the internet." I do agree that traditional spatial distance is not relevant in cyberspace compared to our physical world.


    Still, there exists another form of distance that we observe and encounter: time. It's the time it takes to access digital content. In our real-life experiences as internet users, we perceive this time as our personal "cyber distance." While the internet eliminates the constraints of physical space, the human experience within it is defined by the passage of time. In practical terms, we may not measure distances in clicks as we do in the physical world, but we do gauge our virtual journeys by the seconds it takes to load a webpage or the moments spent waiting for a video to buffer. These seemingly brief intervals are our tangible experience of digital space. These moments where we wait for content to load are the instances when the internet, despite its vastness, becomes tangible to us as users.


    It's this interplay between the instantaneous access to information and the milliseconds of waiting that defines our relationship with the internet. The search engines, acting as the architects of this virtual universe, wield the power to distribute and manipulate space based on our needs, shaping our perception of distance, both in the real and virtual sense. Therefore, as we navigate through the vast landscape of the internet, it's crucial to recognize that while it renders physical distances obsolete, it introduces a new dimension where time becomes the currency. Each click we make carries the weight of these moments, and it's within this subtle dance between immediacy and anticipation that we find ourselves exploring the captivating and ever-expanding world of the internet.


    In light of these reflections, I have created a webpage designed to visually represent this concept of "internet distance" by showcasing the time it takes to access different links. The measured time can be seen as a representation of our "cyber distance," which can vary depending on internet or Wi-Fi settings. The webpage features a stopwatch that initiates when a search begins and stops when the content is fully loaded, providing a tangible representation of the milliseconds of waiting we experience in this digital space.
