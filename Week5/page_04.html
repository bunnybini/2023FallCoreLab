"Human's Eyes - The Wonders of Vision"

1. Light Entry: The process of vision begins when light enters the eye through the cornea, which is the clear, protective outer covering of the eye. The cornea helps to focus light onto the lens.

2. Lens Focus: The lens is a flexible structure located behind the cornea. It adjusts its shape to fine-tune the focus of incoming light onto the retina at the back of the eye. The ability of the lens to change shape is known as accommodation, and it allows us to focus on objects at different distances.

3. Retina and Photoreceptors: The retina is the innermost layer of the eye, akin to the film in a camera. It contains millions of specialized light-sensitive cells called photoreceptors, specifically rods and cones.
