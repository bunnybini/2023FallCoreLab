    This quote made me think deeply because it tells me that making websites doesn't have to be about having big goals or specific plans. What I like about this quote is that it encourages a more relaxed and open approach to making websites. It's like saying, "You don't always need a clear purpose or a set goal when creating a website." This idea feels freeing because it goes against the idea that everything we do online has to have a strict purpose or measurable success.

    The concept of a website is like a treasure trove of random words: room, shelf, plant, or garden, all neatly contained under the umbrella term "Web." At first glance, we might simplistically view a website as a visual representation of links, but the reading in question takes us on a profound journey, delving deep into the very essence of what a "website" truly means.

    Schwulst's metaphor of websites as plants resonates deeply with me. Just as a gardener carefully tends to their plants, we, as creators, nurture our websites with attention and care. We choose the right environment, cultivate content, and foster interactions. Much like planting a seed and watching it grow into a thriving garden, the process of website creation is a labor of love that requires patience, dedication, and a touch of artistry.

    But what truly captivates me is the idea that websites are not static or fixed in purpose. They are, like living organisms, subject to evolution and change. A website can start as a simple idea seed and blossom into something entirely unexpected. It may evolve with time, responding to the shifting tides of user engagement and technological advancements.
