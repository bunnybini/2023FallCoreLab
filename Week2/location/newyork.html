About Manhattan

Brief Information

Manhattan is one of the five boroughs of New York City, located at the core of the city's metropolitan area. Bordered by the Hudson River to the west and the East River to the east, it is an island that stretches approximately 13.4 miles in length and 2.3 miles in width, covering a total area of about 23 square miles. Manhattan is known for its iconic skyline, featuring towering skyscrapers like the Empire State Building and One World Trade Center. It is a cultural, financial, and commercial hub, home to world-renowned institutions such as Central Park, Times Square, Wall Street, and Broadway. Manhattan is characterized by its diverse neighborhoods, each with its unique charm and identity, making it a vibrant and dynamic borough with a rich history and global influence.

City Hall Park is a 8.8-acre public park that surrounds New York City Hall, the seat of the city's government. It's a beautifully landscaped area that provides a green oasis amidst the hustle and bustle of Lower Manhattan. The park features walking paths, benches, statues, and various trees and flowers, making it a popular spot for relaxation and a peaceful escape from the surrounding urban environment.

Architectural Beauty

City Hall Park is surrounded by several architecturally significant buildings. Aside from the iconic New York City Hall, you'll find the Woolworth Building, once the world's tallest skyscraper, and the Surrogate's Courthouse, known for its stunning facade and architectural details.

City Hall Park used to be a place for 'Shaving Parade'
One fun fact about City Hall Park is that it has a rich history and has seen its fair share of notable events. n the 18th century, before it became a park, the site now occupied by City Hall Park was used as a public market known as the "New York Commons." One of the unusual practices at the time was the "Shaving Parade." Every year on May 1st, a parade would take place in which livestock was paraded around the park, and their wool was shaved off right there. This wool was then auctioned to the highest bidder. It was quite a spectacle, and the Shaving Parade was a significant event in early New York City!

So, City Hall Park not only serves as a civic center and green space but also has a colorful history with quirky traditions like the Shaving Parade in its past. It's a fascinating blend of modern urban life and historical charm.

Festivals!! Celebrations!!

Festivals and celebrations held in City Hall Park are vibrant and culturally enriching events that reflect the diversity and dynamism of New York City. Throughout the year, this historic park becomes a focal point for various types of gatherings and festivities.

One of the most noteworthy aspects of festivals and celebrations in City Hall Park is their diversity. The park serves as a canvas for a wide range of cultural events, each representing different communities and traditions. From Chinese New Year festivities to Diwali celebrations, Juneteenth commemorations to LGBTQ+ Pride events, City Hall Park is a symbolic melting pot where the city's rich tapestry of cultures comes to life.

These events showcase music, dance, art, and cuisine from around the world, allowing residents and visitors to immerse themselves in different cultural experiences without leaving the city.
