Charing Cross Road!!

Charing Cross Road, located in the heart of London's West End, is an iconic thoroughfare that has earned a prominent place in the city's literary and cultural history. This celebrated street is renowned for its abundance of bookshops, making it a paradise for bibliophiles from around the world. With a history dating back to the late 19th century, Charing Cross Road has long been associated with the written word, offering a diverse selection of books ranging from rare and antique editions to contemporary bestsellers. Many famous bookstores have called Charing Cross Road home, including the legendary Foyles, a multi-story bookstore that has served generations of readers. The road's bookshops cater to a wide range of interests, ensuring that readers of all genres can find their literary treasures here.
Why is Charing Cross?

The Eleanor Cross

Charing Cross gets its name from the Eleanor Cross, a memorial cross erected by King Edward I in the 13th century in memory of his wife, Eleanor of Castile. Although the original cross no longer stands, a Victorian replica can be found near the station.
